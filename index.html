Meet Naomi. I create websites for individuals like Naomi. I make them responsive so she can use them on-the-go with any of her devices. I make them fast because she also needs it to work on that spotty 3G connection on the train ride to work. And lastly, I make them pretty and useable so she can forget she’s using a website in the first place and focus on getting things done. Isn’t that what’s most important?

I focus on the front-end. The HTML, CSS, and Javascript. The what your eyes can see and what your fingers can swipe part of a website. I focus on making it good, responsive, and fast.

I took care of the design and front-end development. 02
Responsive
Photography

I host all of my photography on photography.derickruiz.com. Of course I wanted the website to be responsive, but one of the most important things to me was that the images that loaded were fast and appropriate for each device. You don't want to load a 1200px image for a device that only needs 480, but that’s hard when you’re trying to showcase high quality photos. I took advantage of the new the new HTML5 picture element, media queries, and picturefill.js, a polyfill for the picture element, to load the most appropriate image for each device. I automatically generated the images at their respective sizes with Grunt. Visit the original website here.

During my time at Lokion I worked with Glasses.com, an ecommerce website specializing in selling glasses and sunglasses, and a team of 5 on their new redesign and the mobification of their website.
